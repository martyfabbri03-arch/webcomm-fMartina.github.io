Pills of Tradition

Cappelletti are a core part of the ancient culinary tradition of Romagna.
They were once considered a humble food, as they could be made using only flour, eggs, and cheese - ingredients easily sourced from the vast countrysides.
They were always prepared within the walls of the home by the Azdore, a Romagnol dialect term that translates to "governess", "housewife", or "female head of the family".
Derived from Latin, it traditionally refers to the woman who governed the house and family, especially in rural areas, taking charge of the kitchen and the management of domestic affairs.
To this day, cappelletti remain one of the main dishes of Romagna's cuisine, enjoyed daily and during festivities such as Christmas and Easter.
